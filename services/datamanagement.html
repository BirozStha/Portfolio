What is a Database and What Does It Do?

The Backbone of Modern Applications
A database is an organized collection of data that allows websites and applications to store, retrieve, manage, and manipulate information efficiently. Whether you're logging into a website, posting on social media, or making an online purchase, a database is working behind the scenes to ensure your data is stored and accessed correctly.

Databases are essential for handling structured and unstructured data, enabling businesses and applications to maintain records, user profiles, transactions, product details, and much more. Without databases, websites and applications would have no way to remember user preferences, store important details, or manage real-time data.
